Santa Teresa

On our second trip to Costa Rica I decided I wanted to include a beach destination because I wanted to include a couple days of down time and relaxation. Spoilers...I didn't have much downtime in Santa Teresa because I was so in love with the area I couldn't stop exploring. A few people who had been to Santa Teresa before highly recommended it, and based on my research it seemed to be more my vibe than Manual Antonio.


Santa Teresa is particulary popular among surfers and it very much has a surf town vibe to it. All of Costa Rica has a low key key, no rush vibe to it; but this is especially true in Santa Teresa. We were there in January which is during the high peak of the tourist season and the beaches never felt particularly crowded. If you've been to Costa Rica or have started looking into going there you have probably heard about wet season vs dry season. This is very apparent in this region. During dry season, basically December thru March, it'll be hot, humid, and dusty. The local roads are all dirt so it's common to see people with bandanas over their face and hair while driving around town to protect themselves from the dust. We haven't been there during the wet season but from my understanding it pours a lot, so you basically trade dusty dirt roads for super muddy ones.

Tips For the Area
  • There are only a few places to stay that are on the beach. We stayed at Hotel Nantipa which was amazing but isn't cheap. There are lots of affordable places that are in the small town.
  • If you're stoked about going to Costa Rica and seeing all the wildlife...you might want to go to a different region. There is a nice national park in the area, Cabo Blanco, which is nice to walk around but it doesn't have the abundant wildlife that we saw in other areas. If you go early in the morning you have a better chance of seeing something. There is also a wildlife preserve in the area that I didn't get a chance to visit, so that might be worth scoping out.
  • If you go during the dry season. Be prepared for the heat, humidity, and dust. I love the heat and humidty and even I was was drenched and out of breath 20 minutes into a morning hike.
  • There's a reason this place is so popular for surfers. The beaches are beautiful, the waves are abundant and strong, and the water is warm. I took my first surfing lesson there and really enjoyed it. Unfortunately now I'm spoiled and don't want to surf anywhere else, definitely not in cold New England.
