Lubec, Maine

Chances are pretty good that you've never heard of Lubec, Maine. It's a small town with only a handful of restaurants and hotels, most of which are only open for half the year. You can literally see Canada from the town and there is a border crossing into Campobello Island so you can even spend the day in Canada if you want. What makes this town worth visiting? Well first of all it's Maine so there is lobster everywhere and it's delicious. Second, it has some amazing cliffs and lighthouses in th area and it doesn't have the crowds of Acadia which is only and hour and half away. Here are some tips for staying in the area.

Tips

  • While this town is no stranger to tourists, it's not like Bar Harbor. There are only a few places to stay and the restaurants will get crowded fast. If you're going with a large group, call ahead to the restaurants in the area to make reservations.
  • Bring your passport! As previously mentioned, Lubec is right on the Canadian border. There are lots of fun things to see on Campobello Island including some harbors with colorful fishing boats.
  • One of the islands in the area is famous for puffins nesting there. There are a few boat tours to see the island but they book up fast, like a year in advance fast. So if this is something you're interested in make sure to make reservations early. You can also sign up to receive notices about cancellations if you're unable to get a reservation.
